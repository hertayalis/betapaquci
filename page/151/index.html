Eddie Johnston was born on November 24, 1935 in Montreal, Canada. He was a NHL goaltender who spent 22 years in the NHL and went on to become a successful manager after retiring. Johnston was a three-time Stanley Cup champion, a two-time Vezina Trophy winner, and a two-time NHL All-Star. He was also inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1982. Johnston's career in management included stints as the head coach of the Hartford Whalers, Pittsburgh Penguins, and Boston Bruins.

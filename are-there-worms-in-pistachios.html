    It’s a worm. Actually, it is very common that pistachios have worms in them. The more research I did on this, I realized that the worm that is commonly found in pistachios are called Navel OrangeWorms. Navel OrangeWorms can be found in almonds and in pistachios.Click to see full answer. Similarly, you may ask, are there bugs in pistachios?Though once considered virtually pest-free, California pistachios are now attacked by a variety of insects, especially the Hemiptera or true bugs. Most are native pests that built resident populations as pistachio orchard acreage increased. These pests are commonly grouped as “small” and “large” bugs.One may also ask, how can you tell if Pistachios are bad? The best way is to smell and look at the pistachios: discard any that have an off smell or appearance; if mold appears, discard the pistachios. Additionally, are closed pistachios safe to eat? Grab a handful of pistachio nuts and you will usually find several with shells that are closed so tightly they cannot be eaten. But soon you might be able to enjoy the snack without this frustration. Closed nuts end up in your nut bowl because sometimes they are speared too.Are pistachio nuts healthy?Pistachio nuts are not only tasty and fun to eat but also super healthy. These edible seeds of the Pistacia vera tree contain healthy fats and are a good source of protein, fiber, and antioxidants. What’s more, they contain several essential nutrients and can aid weight loss and heart and gut health.

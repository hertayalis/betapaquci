    Biography of Stefanos Tsitsipas

    Stefanos Tsitsipas was born in Athens, Greece, on August 12, 1998. Tsitsipas, who was raised by a Greek father and a Russian mother who are both seasoned tennis players, immediately came to terms with the significant impact tennis had on his upbringing.

    His mother formerly held the world’s top junior ranking and competed professionally for the Soviet Union. In addition to Stefanos, his parents had four other children and both were tennis instructors. Their offspring all ended up playing tennis professionally.

    Tsitsipas began working with his father when he was three years old, teaching him how to strike tennis balls with a racquet. Along with his family, he frequently watched tennis matches on television.

    While Stefanos was growing up, he also participated in other sports, but in the end he decided to concentrate on a potential tennis professional career. Later, Tsitsipas said that his father did not “make the decision for him,” and that at the age of nine, he made the decision to pursue this career.

    As of August 2021, Stefanos was ranked as high as world number 3 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP), becoming the first Greek to do so at the age of eighteen.

    The 2019 Association of Tennis Professionals Finals champion, Stefanos Tsitsipas, was the event’s youngest winner in the event’s eighteen-year history.

    He lost to Novak Djokovic in the French Open final in 2021. He also reached the semifinals of the Australian Open three times. He has won eight lifetime championships and eight games for Greece.

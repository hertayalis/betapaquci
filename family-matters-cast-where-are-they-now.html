    The 1989 sitcom “Family Matters” became a staple of American television, running for nine seasons and leaving a lasting impression on its audience. The show centered around the Winslow family and their nerdy neighbor Steve Urkel, played by Jaleel White. Over the years, fans have often wondered what happened to the beloved cast members after the show ended. This article will delve into the lives of the “Family Matters” cast and where they are now.

    Reginald VelJohnson (Carl Winslow)

    Reginald VelJohnson portrayed the patriarch of the Winslow family, Carl Winslow, a role that made him a household name. Since the show’s conclusion, VelJohnson has continued acting, with appearances in TV shows like “Hart of Dixie,” “Mike & Molly,” and “Brooklyn Nine-Nine.” He has also done voice work for animated series and has been involved in stage productions.

    Jo Marie Payton (Harriette Winslow)

    Jo Marie Payton, who played the matriarch Harriette Winslow, left the show during its final season but didn’t slow down in her career. She has appeared in numerous television shows, including “The Proud Family” as the voice of Suga Mama, and had guest roles on shows like “Will & Grace” and “Mann & Wife.”

    Jaleel White (Steve Urkel)

    Jaleel White’s portrayal of Steve Urkel made him an iconic character in the ’90s. Post-“Family Matters,” White has had a diverse career, including participating in “Dancing with the Stars,” hosting the game show “Total Blackout,” and acting in series such as “Grown Ups” and “Me, Myself & I.” He has also ventured into writing and producing.

    Kellie Shanygne Williams (Laura Winslow)

    Kellie Shanygne Williams played Laura Winslow, the object of Urkel’s affection. After the show, Williams took a step back from acting to focus on family and community work. She has made occasional television appearances and is involved in various philanthropic efforts.

    Darius McCrary (Eddie Winslow)

    Darius McCrary portrayed Eddie Winslow, the eldest Winslow child. McCrary continued his acting career with roles in “The Young and the Restless” and “Anger Management.” He has also pursued a music career and has been involved in theater productions.

    Jamie Foxworth (Judy Winslow)

    Jamie Foxworth appeared as the youngest Winslow daughter, Judy, until her character was written off the show. Foxworth faced personal struggles after her departure but has since worked on rebuilding her life. She has made appearances on reality TV and has spoken openly about her experiences in the entertainment industry.

    Bryton James (Richie Crawford)

    Bryton James played Richie Crawford, the Winslow’s young cousin. James transitioned to soap operas, becoming a regular on “The Young and the Restless.” He has also done voice work for animated series like “The Vampire Diaries” and won multiple Daytime Emmy Awards.

    Rosetta LeNoire (Mother Winslow)

    Rosetta LeNoire, who played the beloved Mother Winslow, passed away in 2002. She left behind a legacy of theater work and was recognized for her efforts to promote racial equality in the arts.

    Telma Hopkins (Rachel Crawford)

    Telma Hopkins, who played Rachel Crawford, Harriette’s sister, continued her acting career with roles in “Half & Half,” “Are We There Yet?” and “Dead to Me.” She has also made guest appearances on various television shows and remains active in the entertainment industry.

    Shawn Harrison (Waldo Geraldo Faldo)

    Shawn Harrison portrayed Waldo Geraldo Faldo, Eddie’s best friend known for his comedic timing. Harrison has since had roles in TV series like “Girl Meets World” and has worked behind the scenes in the costume department for film and television.

    Michelle Thomas (Myra Monkhouse)

    Michelle Thomas played Myra Monkhouse, Steve Urkel’s girlfriend. Tragically, Thomas passed away in 1998 due to a rare form of stomach cancer. She is remembered for her roles in “Family Matters” and “The Young and the Restless.”

    Orlando Brown (3J Winslow)

    Orlando Brown appeared as 3J Winslow, the Winslow’s adopted son, in the later seasons. Brown went on to have a successful career as a child actor, including a role in “That’s So Raven.” However, he has faced legal and personal issues in recent years.

    FAQ Section

    Did any of the “Family Matters” cast continue to work together after the show?

    While the cast members have pursued individual careers, there have been occasional reunions on screen, such as Jaleel White and Darius McCrary appearing together in an episode of “McCrary’s TV show “Monogamy.”

    Has there been a “Family Matters” reunion or reboot?

    There has not been an official “Family Matters” reboot, but the cast has reunited for interviews and special events, sparking nostalgia among fans.

    What impact did “Family Matters” have on television?

    “Family Matters” was one of the longest-running African American sitcoms and helped pave the way for future shows featuring predominantly black casts. It also introduced the world to the iconic character Steve Urkel, who became a cultural phenomenon.

    Conclusion

    The cast of “Family Matters” has experienced a wide range of successes and challenges since the show ended. While some have continued to thrive in the entertainment industry, others have taken different paths or faced personal difficulties. The legacy of “Family Matters” lives on, not only through the continued work of its cast but also through the lasting impact it had on its audience and the television landscape. The show’s message that family truly matters resonates to this day, and the cast members, each in their own way, continue to contribute to the fabric of entertainment and culture.
